What Is an FDE?
More Than AI Fluency.

A Field Deployment Engineer (FDE) connects enterprise problems, AI capability, operational constraints, and accountable delivery outcomes. The role is broader than a product demo and more outcome-oriented than simply implementing a ticket.

01 / DEFINITION

WHAT IS AN FDE?

An FDE owns the path from problem to operational result.

An FDE turns an ambiguous enterprise need into an executable AI use case, then carries it through pilot, production, acceptance, and sustained use under real data, workflow, authority, and organizational constraints.

Why does the role matter now?

AI capability is easier to access, but enterprise delivery is not automatically easier. The hard parts remain: unclear problems, unusable data, unowned exceptions, weak value measurement, and teams unwilling to change the way work gets done. An FDE incorporates those conditions into the solution instead of demonstrating a prototype that only works outside the field.

02 / RESPONSIBILITY

WHAT DOES AN FDE DO?

Six responsibilities define the work.

Diagnose the real problem, select the right use case, design the solution, control the risk, drive delivery, and prove that the system creates an outcome the business can accept and sustain.

01 / DIAGNOSE

Diagnose the business

Interview stakeholders, map the real workflow, and establish pain, frequency, cost, baseline, and available evidence.

02 / PRIORITIZE

Choose the right use case

Balance value, data readiness, risk, complexity, sponsorship, and adoption friction before selecting what to build first.

03 / DESIGN

Design the operating solution

Specify inputs, models, knowledge, tools, permissions, human approvals, exceptions, outputs, and observability.

04 / PROVE

Deliver and prove value

Use baselines, pilots, quality gates, adoption measures, and business metrics to produce traceable acceptance evidence.

03 / CAPABILITY

ONEX FIVE-DIMENSION MODEL

The OneX model examines five connected capabilities.

FDE capability is not excellence with one tool. It is the ability to diagnose the business, judge use cases, construct a solution, control risk, and execute delivery as one accountable system.

01 / DIA

Business Diagnosis

Find the real workflow, decision owners, baseline, constraints, and evidence behind the request.

02 / SCN

Use-Case Judgment

Know what deserves to be built first—and what should not yet be automated.

03 / SOL

Solution Design

Turn a requirement into an executable, observable, testable, and reversible workflow.

04 / SAFE

Risk Boundaries

Control data, authority, approvals, commitments, auditability, and failure recovery.

05 / DEL

Delivery Execution

Move beyond the demo into pilot, production, acceptance, adoption, and sustained use.

04 / COMPARISON

ROLE BOUNDARIES

How is an FDE different from sales engineering, product, or software engineering?

The decisive difference is not whether the person can code. It is the accountability boundary: an FDE is responsible for whether the solution survives the enterprise field and produces an accepted result.

RolePrimary objectiveTypical outputsAccountability for field outcome
FDETurn AI capability into an operational, accepted business outcomeDiagnosis, pilot, workflow, controls, acceptance evidenceSpans problem definition, technical choices, delivery, and adoption
Sales engineerProve product fit for a customer needDemo, solution brief, technical clarificationOften concentrated before contract or handoff
Product managerDecide what a repeatable product should solveRoadmap, requirements, product metricsOwns product direction; may not own an individual deployment
Software / ML engineerBuild reliable software, data, or model systemsCode, models, services, infrastructureOwns technical quality within the team's designed boundary

Everyone starts at Foundation. No level can be skipped. A full assessment unlocks the next level only with at least 85 overall and 70 in every module. Random practice sets are for learning and do not unlock progression.

06 / BOUNDARY

WHAT THIS RESULT MEANS

A high online score does not certify someone as an FDE.

This site is a public FDE capability challenge and awareness tool. It measures knowledge and scenario judgment; it is not formal graduation, certification, or proof of real-project capability.

What would a formal capability review still need?

Real project artifacts, a working solution, acceptance evidence, live challenge and defense, data and authority controls, stakeholder feedback, and a review of sustained operating results. An online score may be one input, but it cannot replace field evidence.
